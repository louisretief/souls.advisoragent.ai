## Purpose

This file gives you — the AI agent — two things:

1. **A normalization map** to group inconsistently named CRM workflows into canonical categories
2. **Industry benchmarks** showing real workflow volume data across advisory firms, so you can identify what is normal, what is missing, and where a firm's operational investment is concentrated

Use this data to enrich your analysis, benchmark what you observe in the CRM, and identify gaps where a firm has low or no activity in high-volume categories.

## Category Definitions & Keyword Normalization Map

Use the keywords below to map raw CRM workflow names to canonical categories. When a workflow name contains one of these keywords, classify it under the associated category.

---

### 1. Tax Planning & Prep
**Canonical name:** Tax Planning & Prep
**Keywords:** Tax, Tax Review, Tax Planning, Tax Season, Tax Loss Harvesting, HSA, Taxable, CPA, Accountant, Certified Professional Accountant, Accounting, Qualified Charitable Distribution, QCD

**What to look for:**
- Is there a structured tax prep workflow or is it ad hoc?
- Are QCDs and tax loss harvesting being systematically tracked?
- Is there a seasonal workflow that activates before April and year-end?

## Gap Detection Reference

Use this table when a category is absent or underrepresented in a firm's CRM data relative to industry benchmarks.

| Missing Category | Likely Implication | Suggested Action |
|-----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Tax Planning & Prep | Highest-volume category industry-wide. Absence suggests significant untracked work or missed systematization opportunity. | Prioritize building a tax prep workflow immediately. |
| Client Meetings | If low, either meetings aren't happening (client experience risk) or aren't being tracked (data blindspot). | Implement meeting prep + post-meeting follow-up workflow. |
| Client Onboarding | Absence or inconsistency here is the #1 predictor of early client attrition. | Standardize a single onboarding workflow before all else. |
| Account Opening | If low relative to AUM, paperwork is likely being done outside the CRM — compliance and efficiency risk. | Map current account opening steps and systematize. |
| Investment Management | RMD tracking absence is a compliance risk, especially for clients over 73. | Audit client ages and implement RMD tracking immediately. |
| Compliance & Ops | Absence suggests ADV and IPS processes are not systematized — regulatory risk. | Build compliance calendar workflow as a priority. |
| Insurance Service | Commonly neglected. Often represents missed revenue and planning gaps. | Schedule annual insurance reviews for all clients. |

## Notes for the Agent

- Workflow names in CRMs are **never standardized**. "New Client Setup", "Client Onboarding 2024", and "NB Onboarding" are all the same thing. Use fuzzy keyword matching, not exact matching.
- A firm with **high volume in a category but no structured workflow** is operating on tribal knowledge — high risk, high optimization opportunity.
- A firm with **low volume in a high-benchmark category** either isn't doing that work (service gap) or isn't tracking it (data gap). Both matter.
- The goal is not to criticize — it is to show the advisor where their team's energy is going and where the highest ROI improvements live.
