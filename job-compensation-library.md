## Purpose

This file gives you — the AI agent — current, research-backed compensation benchmarks for common advisory firm roles. Use this data to:

1. **Include realistic salary ranges** in every job posting you produce
2. **Adjust ranges by location** using the regional multipliers below
3. **Frame compensation competitively** — advisory firms are competing for talent against larger financial institutions

All figures are in USD and reflect 2025 market data. Ranges represent base salary. Total compensation (including bonus, benefits, and profit sharing) is typically 10–20% higher.

---

## Regional Cost-of-Living Multipliers

Apply these multipliers to the national baseline ranges below based on the firm's location.

| Region | Example Cities | Multiplier |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| High cost — Northeast | New York, Boston, Washington DC | 1.25–1.40x |
| High cost — West Coast | San Francisco, Seattle, Los Angeles | 1.20–1.35x |
| Above average | Chicago, Denver, Austin, Miami | 1.05–1.15x |
| National average | Dallas, Atlanta, Phoenix, Charlotte | 1.00x |
| Below average — Midwest | Columbus, Indianapolis, Kansas City | 0.85–0.95x |
| Below average — South/Rural | Memphis, Omaha, smaller markets | 0.80–0.90x |

**How to use:** Take the national baseline range and multiply both ends by the appropriate regional factor. Example: A Client Service Representative baseline of $50,000–$70,000 in San Francisco becomes approximately $60,000–$95,000.

|-----------------|-------------------|------------------------|

## Benefits Benchmarks

Use these as the standard benefits section in job postings. Adjust based on firm size context.

**Standard benefits package (most RIA firms):**
- Health insurance (employer covers 70–100% of individual premium)
- 401(k) with employer match (3–4% is standard; top firms offer 5–6%)
- 15–20 days PTO plus holidays
- Professional development budget ($1,000–$3,000/year)
- Licensing and certification support (exam fees, study materials)

**Competitive differentiators (used by top talent-destination firms):**
- Parental leave (4–12 weeks paid)
- Profit sharing or equity participation
- Remote/hybrid flexibility
- Wellness stipends ($500–$1,500/year)
- Continuing education reimbursement beyond licensing
- Conference attendance (FPA, NAPFA, Schwab IMPACT)

## Notes for the Agent

- Always present compensation as a **range**, never a single number. Ranges signal flexibility and are standard practice in advisory firm hiring.
- Always note that **total compensation exceeds base salary** — bonuses, benefits, and profit sharing are meaningful in this industry.
- When the firm's location is provided, **apply the regional multiplier** before presenting ranges. A $55,000 baseline role in New York City should be presented as $68,000–$77,000.
- The advisory firm talent market is **tight and competitive**. Frame compensation as a strength, not just a number — top candidates evaluate culture, growth path, and mission alongside salary.
- Never present compensation ranges that are **below market** without flagging it. If a firm's location and size suggest they need to pay more to compete, say so diplomatically.
