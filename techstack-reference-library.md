## HOW TO USE THIS LIBRARY

This library is a reference data source for the Tech Stack Analyzer agent. When a firm names a tool, look it up here to understand: what category it belongs to, what journey stages it serves, how it rates in the market, what it integrates with, and what gaps it may leave.

Market share = % of all surveyed advisors using the tool (not % within category).  
Trend arrows: ↑ gaining share, ↓ losing share, → stable, ★ new entrant

## CATEGORY 1: CRM (Client Relationship Management)

**What it is:** The operational backbone of an advisory firm. Manages client records, contact history, tasks, workflows, notes, and increasingly serves as the hub connecting all other tools. The 2026 T3 survey found 91.08% market penetration — the highest of any category.

**Journey stages:** 2 (Prospect), 3 (Onboarding), 4 (Planning), 5 (Ongoing Service), 6 (Transition)

**Why it matters:** Without a CRM, firms have no systematic way to ensure consistent service delivery, track client interactions, or automate follow-up. Advisors who lack a CRM are literally flying blind. The 2-week contact rule during onboarding (don't let 2 weeks pass without client contact) is only enforceable with a CRM.

**Key integrations to look for:** Financial planning software, portfolio management, e-signature, AI notetakers, scheduling tools, document management, custodial platforms

**Market trends (2023-2026):**
- Overall penetration: 96.46% (2023) → 86.33% (2025) → 91.08% (2026) — recovered after unusual 2025 dip
- Category rating: 7.62 → 7.21 → 7.70 — significant improvement
- AI integration is transforming the category — notetakers pushing into CRM territory
- All-in-one platforms (Advyzon, Orion) blurring lines between CRM and portfolio management

## CATEGORY 2: FINANCIAL PLANNING SOFTWARE

**What it is:** The core analytical engine for creating comprehensive financial plans. Covers retirement projections, cash flow, goals-based planning, scenario analysis. 83.3% market penetration — the second most universally used category.

**Journey stages:** 4 (Financial Planning & Implementation) primarily; modules extend to all planning subspecialties

**Why it matters:** This is where the advisor's core intellectual value is delivered. Without financial planning software, advisors are either investment managers only or operating on spreadsheets. The non-adopters (16.7%) are likely pure asset managers.

**Key integrations:** CRM (two-way data sync), account aggregation, client portal, tax planning, estate planning, custodial

**Market trends (2023-2026):**
- Penetration stable at 83-85% for 4 years
- eMoney surged to market leader (35.6%) — passed MoneyGuidePro (was at 33% in 2024)
- RightCapital growing steadily, especially younger/smaller firms
- FP Alpha emerging as AI-powered supplement (not replacement) to core platforms
- Asset-Map used as visual supplement by 72%+ of users alongside a core platform
- Trend: AI-generated advice content integrated directly into planning workflows

## CATEGORY 12: WORKFLOW TOOLS

**What it is:** Structured process automation — defined sequences of tasks, timelines, responsibilities, and communications triggered by specific events (new client, review meeting, onboarding, life event). Explosive growth: 2.64% penetration (2024) → 19.28% (2026).

## MARKET-WIDE INSIGHTS & PATTERNS

### The Integration Stack Problem
The average advisory firm uses 5-8 technology solutions. Each integration point is a potential failure — data not syncing, manual re-entry required, workflows breaking. The firms with the cleanest tech stacks have either:
1. Chosen an all-in-one platform (Advyzon, Orion) and accept some capability tradeoffs, OR
2. Carefully mapped integrations between best-of-breed tools and invested in keeping them connected

### The Underpenetration Problem — Categories Firms Should Have But Often Don't
- **Cybersecurity:** Only 21.7% — regulatory exposure, client trust risk
- **Healthcare planning:** Only 7.26% — major advice gap for retiree-serving firms
- **AI notetaking:** 42.9% — but should be 90%+ given clear ROI
- **Retirement income planning:** 18.5% — underpenetrated for firms serving pre-retirees
- **Workflow tools:** 19.3% — most firms have no structured process automation

### The Rating Cliff — Tools Below Satisfaction Threshold (Under 7.0)
These tools are causing active user dissatisfaction and represent migration risk:
- Morningstar ByAllAccounts: 5.85 — critical
- Elements: 5.53 — critical
- Naviplan: 6.79 — approaching
- SmartAsset AMP: 5.22 — critical
- Envestnet/Tamarac CRM: 6.96 — third consecutive year below 7.0
- RIA in a Box: 6.81 — declining
- Microsoft Dynamics: 6.29 — below threshold

### Rapidly Growing Tools to Watch (2025→2026)
- **Jump (AI notetaking):** 8.36% → 22.68% — fastest growth in survey history
- **Zocks (AI notetaking):** 1.74% → 10.22%
- **TaxStatus:** 1.46% → 9.22%
- **Altruist:** Consistent gains across custodian, PM, trading, billing
- **Quivr (CRM overlay):** Growing with extraordinary 9.47 rating
- **Greenboard (compliance):** 0.33% → 1.45% with 8.43 rating
- **Hadrius (AI compliance):** 0.28% → 1.14% with 8.15 rating
- **Wealth.com (estate):** 7.61% → 13.73%
- **Holistiplan estate module:** New → 9.94% in first year

### The AI Transformation Wave
AI is entering the advisory tech stack in three distinct ways:
1. **Meeting intelligence** (Jump, Zocks): Transcription → summary → CRM action items
2. **Planning intelligence** (FP Alpha, Holistiplan, TaxStatus): AI-analyzed documents → advice recommendations
3. **Compliance intelligence** (Hadrius, Greenboard): AI-monitored communications + compliance workflows

Firms that have adopted none of these are operating with a widening capability gap relative to AI-enabled competitors.

### Business Model Patterns
From the T3 2026 data:
- **Fee-only firms:** Prefer RightCapital, Advyzon, FP Alpha, Holistiplan; avoid Tamarac
- **Dually-registered:** Prefer Redtail, MoneyGuidePro, Asset-Map
- **Larger firms ($3M+ revenue):** Salesforce FSC, Addepar, Orion, eMoney
- **Newer/smaller firms:** Wealthbox, RightCapital, Altruist
- **Enterprise/family office:** Salesforce, Addepar, Bloomberg Terminal
